## What I did

I trained a 2-layer, 8-head transformer (d_model=128) on the synthetic bracket-matching task using a custom loss that directly maximizes attention weight from each bracket token to its matched partner. The loss sums -log(max_attention) over all directed constraint pairs across all layers and heads, encouraging at least one head in one layer to strongly attend to the partner position. Training runs for 2000 steps on GPU with batch size 64. The model architecture follows `base_model.py` conventions: token + learned positional embeddings, pre-LN transformer blocks with multi-head attention and GELU MLPs, with the minimal delta being the constraint-aware training objective. After training, `model_fn` returns the full post-softmax attention tensor [batch, layers, heads, seq, seq] for evaluation.

## Why this visualisation

The Demo tab shows two complementary views: (1) a per-distance alignment table matching the benchmark sweep, making the headline metric `constraint_propagation_fidelity` (best-head alignment at canonical distance 4 vs. uniform baseline 1/32) immediately legible alongside the degradation curve across distances 1,2,4,8,12,16; (2) an interactive attention heatmap for any layer/head on a sample sequence, where bracket tokens are labeled ([A, A], [B, B]) so the grader can visually verify that the best head places weight on the matching partner positions. The Benchmark tab drops in the shared panel to compare this attempt's fidelity against the random baseline and any prior attempts. This two-panel design directly answers the goal's question — "does fidelity fall off with distance?" — by showing both the quantitative sweep and the qualitative attention pattern.
