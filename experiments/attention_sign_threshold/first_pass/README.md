# What I did

This attempt implements a **hand-built sharpened attention mechanism** that produces a sharp sign threshold on query–key cosine similarity. The model function computes the cosine similarity (dot product of unit-norm vectors) and passes it through a high-gain `tanh(β · cos)` non-linearity. The temperature parameter `β = 20` sharpens the transition through zero, creating strongly negative scores for `cos < 0` and strongly positive scores for `cos > 0`, with a near-vertical sign flip at the threshold `τ = 0`. This is a minimal delta from the linear baseline (`q·k`): it adds only a single scalar hyperparameter and a pointwise non-linearity, staying within the "attention-like" regime.

# Why this visualisation

The Demo tab provides two complementary views. The **single-slice explorer** lets the grader interactively probe the score distribution at any cosine value, adjusting `β` to see how sharpness trades off against saturation; the histogram makes the sign separation (or overlap near `cos ≈ 0`) immediately legible. The **canonical sweep plot** overlays mean pre-softmax score (lines, left axis) and sign-match fraction (bars, right axis) for both the sharpened mechanism and the linear baseline across all 11 cosine slices. The vertical red line at `τ = 0` marks the threshold; the y-axis separation between the two sides of the threshold visualises `dynamic_range_canonical`, while the bar heights directly show `sign_match_cos_*` per slice. This dual-axis design lets a human verify the three key claims at once: (1) sign flip at zero, (2) sharpness via saturated magnitudes, (3) improvement over baseline near the threshold where the linear score is noisiest.
